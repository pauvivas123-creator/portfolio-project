Post 1:
- Platform: LinkedIn
- 12 hours.

- We sell email intelligence, so take this LinkedIn outbound advice with a grain of salt. But, if you're automating LI DMs, here's something I do that makes it work a whole lot better:

I've got a HeyReach account.

I know I know... I kinda hate myself for automating on LI.

BUT... I do two things that make it not as terrible for the recipient.

1) How do I actually send LI dms?

Well.. I usually do it like a text.

Broken up thoughts. 1 by 1... 

I do LinkedIn the same way. What would be a single email message is broken up into 3.

2) I use LinkedIn automations as a way to distribute content... I just share something I think would be helpful. No ask.

Message one:

Did you see our new email benchmarking report

*send & delay*

Message two: Here's the link, if not.
~link~

*send & delay*

Message three: Figured it would be easier if I just sent it over. Would love to hear what you think though.

## Insights
- Breaking messages into smaller parts increases engagement
- LinkedIn outreach works better when it feels like a natural conversation
- Providing value first (without asking) improves response rates
- Automation can work if it mimics human communication patterns

Why?
